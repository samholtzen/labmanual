# DNA Ligation with T4 DNA Ligase

## Materials

- Digested and cleaned vector DNA
- Digested and cleaned insert DNA
- T4 DNA ligase
- 10X T4 DNA ligase buffer
- Nuclease-free water

## Protocol

1. Thaw the T4 DNA ligase and T4 buffer at room temperature. The buffer may have crystals floating when cold, but vortexing and warming will redissolve them. Do not vortext the T4 DNA ligase.

1. Combine insert:vector DNA in a 3:1 molar ratio in a microcentrifuge tube. Use [this calculator](https://nebiocalculator.neb.com/#!/ligation#!%2F) to determine the mass of each fragment to add to the reaction.

1. Always include a vector only control with no insert to determine if there was incomplete cutting of backbone.

2. Add 2 µL of DNA ligase buffer to the microcentrifuge tube.

3. Add nuclease-free water to bring the volume to 19 µL.

4. Add 1 µL of T4 DNA ligase

5. Incubate at room temperature for 2 hours
6. Heat inactivate at 65 ºC for 10 minutes.
7. Store at -20 ºC until ready for transformation.

As a reference, a typical ligation reaction is shown below:
