# Restriction Digest

## Materials

- Plasmid or linear DNA
- Restriction enzyme
- Compatible restriction enzyme buffer (usually rCutSmart)
- Nuclease-free water

## Protocol

1. If you are digesting DNA with more than one restriction enzyme, ensure that your buffer is compatible with both using [this chart](https://www.neb.com/en-us/tools-and-resources/usage-guidelines/nebuffer-performance-chart-with-restriction-enzymes?srsltid=AfmBOoohQHuFfPyOZgvVAL2z5X4xYZN3m95GNQnkzz7IdvLLxeSvnJ3t) from NEB. In addition, ensure that the enzymes are both active at the same incubation temperature.
2. Thaw both the restriction enzyme(s) and buffer on ice.
3. Add 1-2 µg of the plasmid or linear DNA to be digested to PCR tubes.
4. Add the following reagents to the tube that contains the DNA:

    | Component | Volume (µL) | Final Concentration |
    |---|---|---|
    | Digest buffer (10X) | 5 | - |
    | Nuclease-free water | to 49 | — |
    | Restriction enzyme(s) | 1 | 10 U |
    | **Total volume** | **50**

5. Load the PCR tubes into a thermocycler and set to the incubation temperature found in the chart in step 1.
6. ***Optional***: Heat inactivate the restriction enzymes with a 5 minute incubation at 60ºC. Some enzymes, like BamHI, cannot be heat inactivated.
7. Store at -20ºC or continue to gel electrophoresis for purification or visualization.
