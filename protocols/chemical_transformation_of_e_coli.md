# Chemical transformation of E. coli

Adapted from NEB’s “High Efficiency Transformation Protocol” and “5 Minute Transformation Protocol”

### Components

- NEB 5-alpha: -80 freezer in lab
- Heat block: bacterial bench
- Ice: Autoclave room
- Ice buckets: just use any styrofoam bucket
- SOC: cabinet with PBS
- Plates: back of the cold room

### Protocol:

1. Thaw a tube of NEB 5-alpha Competent *E. coli* cells on ice until the last ice crystals disappear. Mix gently and carefully pipette 50 µl of cells into a transformation tube on ice.
2. Add 1-5 µl containing 1 pg-100 ng of plasmid DNA to the cell mixture. Carefully flick the tube 4-5 times to mix cells and DNA. **Do not vortex.**
3. Place the mixture on ice for 5 minutes. Do not mix.
4. Heat shock at exactly 42°C for exactly 30 seconds. Do not mix.
5. Place on ice for 5 minutes. Do not mix.
6. Pipette 950 µl of room temperature SOC into the mixture.
7. Place at 37°C for 60 minutes. Shake vigorously (250 rpm).
8. Warm selection plates to 37°C.
9. Mix the cells thoroughly by flicking the tube and inverting.
10. Spread 50µL of recovery mixture onto a selection plate and incubate at 37 ºC overnight. Alternatively, at 30ºC for 36 hours or room temperature over the weekend.
11. Keep the rest of the recovery mixture in the fridge for plating if all did not go well.
